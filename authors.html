Call for Papers

IEEE AFRICON 2027 invites submissions of original, unpublished research papers from researchers, engineers, and practitioners from universities, industry, and research institutions worldwide. The conference theme is "Harnessing Emerging Technologies for Africa's sustainable development".

Conference Tracks

Submissions should be directed to one of the six thematic tracks below. Each track covers a focused area of engineering and technology with relevance to Africa's development agenda. Authors should select the track that best aligns with the primary contribution of their paper.

Paper Submission

All papers must be submitted electronically through the Microsoft CMT paper management system. Submitted papers must be original work not previously published or currently under review for any other conference or journal.

Papers should be written in English and must comply with the IEEE two-column format. The maximum paper length is 8 pages (including references). Authors may purchase up to 2 additional pages at an extra charge.

Author Guidelines

Paper Format

  • Papers must be formatted using the IEEE two-column conference template.
  • Templates are available for Microsoft Word and LaTeX.
  • Maximum length: 8 pages including all figures, tables, and references.
  • Up to 2 additional pages may be purchased during registration.
  • All submissions must be in PDF format.

Review Process

All papers will undergo rigorous double-blind peer review. Authors must ensure their submissions are anonymized — remove author names, affiliations, and acknowledgements from the submitted manuscript.

Publication

Accepted and presented papers will be submitted for inclusion in the IEEE Xplore Digital Library. Authors of accepted papers must register for the conference and present their work in person (or virtually).
