Welcome Message from Technical Program Chair — IEEE AFRICON 2027

Dear Authors and Distinguished Participants,

It is my honour to welcome you to the 2027 IEEE AFRICON Conference, to be held at GIMPA, Accra, Ghana. This year’s technical program reflects outstanding scholarship, innovation, and collaboration across the electrical and computer engineering disciplines, aligned with the theme: “Harnessing Emerging Technologies for Africa’s Sustainable Development”.

We are pleased to announce that paper submissions are now open. We invite researchers from universities, industry partners, and research institutions across Africa and around the globe to submit their finest work for peer review.

All submitted papers will undergo rigorous blind peer review by a diverse and experienced team of reviewers drawn from across the world. Accepted papers will be published in the IEEE Xplore Digital Library, ensuring global visibility and impact.

We thank the reviewers, track chairs, and technical committee members for their diligence, professionalism, and commitment to maintaining high academic standards throughout the review process.

We encourage you to take advantage of the technical sessions, engage in meaningful dialogue, and build networks that advance engineering research and innovation on the continent.

We wish you a productive, enriching, and memorable AFRICON 2027 experience.

Ing Prof Issaka Yakubu
Technical Program Chair
2027 IEEE AFRICON Conference

Message from the General Chair — IEEE AFRICON 2027

On behalf of the Organizing Committee, I would like to welcome you to the 19th edition of the IEEE AFRICON 2027 Conference, to be held at GIMPA, Accra, Ghana, from September 23rd to 25th, 2027. The conference theme is “Harnessing Emerging Technologies for Africa’s Sustainable Development”.

The conference provides a platform for students, researchers, and industry professionals worldwide to meet, network, and pursue collaboration opportunities. It also serves as a forum to share research findings, exchange ideas, and foster innovation. Thematic research areas include Artificial and Computational Intelligence, Cloud Computing and Data Analytics, Wireless Communication Systems, Antennas, Microwave Systems and Propagation Models, Integrated Sensing & Communication and IoT, Power Engineering & Energy Systems, and Cybersecurity.

The 19th edition of IEEE AFRICON 2027 continues the proud tradition of fostering world-class research across the continent. All submitted papers will undergo rigorous blind peer review by a diverse and experienced team of reviewers drawn from across the globe.

In addition to plenary presentations, the conference will feature keynote speeches and invited talks delivered by leading experts in electrical and computer engineering. IEEE Region 8 has also organized several workshops, plenary sessions, and presentations to provide participants with a rich and engaging technical experience.

I would like to express my heartfelt appreciation for the hard work, dedication, and commitment of all committees, including the Technical Program Committee, Local Organizing Committee, Publication Committee, and other supporting teams, whose tireless efforts are essential to the success of this conference.

In closing, as we work toward harnessing emerging technologies for Africa’s sustainable development, let us continue to explore new ways of advancing humanity through innovation and collaboration as a global research community.

Dr. George Eduful, SMIEEE
General Chair

About IEEE AFRICON 2027

The conference will be organized by the IEEE South Africa Section in collaboration with the IEEE Ghana Section. The event will be hosted at GIMPA, Accra, Ghana from the 23rd to 25th of September 2027, under the theme “Innovating Africa’s Future Through Technology”.

This conference will feature world-class plenary speakers, technical symposiums, industry and academic panels, workshops, tutorials, and invited tracks on emerging technologies. IEEE AFRICON 2027 is co-sponsored by IEEE Region 8 and the IEEE South Africa Section and is the flagship conference for the African continent.

This will be the 19th Edition of the biennial conference which was previously held in Nairobi, Kenya (1983), Abidjan, Ivory Coast (1987), Mbabane, Swaziland (1992), Stellenbosch, South Africa (1996), Cape Town, South Africa (1999), George, South Africa (2002), Gaborone, Botswana (2004), Windhoek, Namibia (2007), Nairobi, Kenya (2009), Livingston, Zambia (2011), Mauritius (2013), Addis Ababa, Ethiopia (2015), Cape Town, South Africa (2017), Accra, Ghana (2019), Arusha, Tanzania (2021), Nairobi, Kenya (2023), and Polokwane, South Africa (2025).
