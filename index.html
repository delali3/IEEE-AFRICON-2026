Message from the Technical Program Chair — 2026 IEEE AFRICON Conference

Dear Authors, Researchers, and Distinguished Participants,

It is my honour to welcome you to the 2026 IEEE AFRICON Conference in Kigali, Rwanda. This year's technical program reflects outstanding scholarship, innovation, and collaboration across the electrical and computer engineering disciplines.

We are pleased to announce that paper submissions are now open. We invite researchers from universities, industry partners, and research institutions across Africa and around the globe to submit their finest work for peer review.

All submitted papers will undergo rigorous blind peer review by a diverse and experienced team of reviewers drawn from across the world. Accepted papers will be published in the IEEE Xplore Digital Library.

We extend our heartfelt appreciation to the reviewers, track chairs, and technical committee members for their diligence and professionalism throughout the selection process.

Whether you are attending physically or participating online, we encourage you to take full advantage of the technical sessions, engage in meaningful dialogue, and build networks that advance engineering research on the continent.

We wish you a productive, enriching, and memorable AFRICON 2026 experience.

Welcome Message from General Chair

On behalf of the Organizing Committee, I would like to welcome you to the 18th edition of the IEEE AFRICON 2026 conference to be held at GIMPA in Kigali, Rwanda from November 18th to 20th, 2026. The conference theme is “Innovating Africa’s Future Through Technology”.

The conference provides a platform for students, researchers and the industry worldwide to meet, network and pursue collaboration opportunities. The conference will also provide a forum to share research findings and ideas. The thematic research areas include Artificial and Computational Intelligence, Cloud Computing and Data Analytics, Wireless Communication Systems, Antennas, Microwave Systems and Propagation Models, Integrated Sensing & Communication and IoT, Power Engineering & Energy Systems and Cybersecurity.

The 18th edition of IEEE Africon 2026 continues the proud tradition of fostering world-class research across the continent. All submitted papers will be subjected to blind peer review. Papers will be reviewed by a diverse and experienced team of reviewers drawn across the world.

In addition to plenary presentations, the conference will consist of keynote speakers and invited presentations by leading experts in electrical and computer engineering.

IEEE Region 8 has also organized a couple of workshops, plenary sessions and presentations one may look forward to.

I would like to express my heartfelt appreciation for the sacrifice, hard work and dedication of all the committees which are working tirelessly to ensure the success of the conference, the Technical Program Committee, Local Organising Committee, and the publication committee and other teams.

In closing, as we innovate Africa’s future through technology, let us explore new ways of advancing humanity as a global research community.

Prof. [TBD], Ph.D., FIEEE

About IEEE AFRICON 2026

The conference will be organized by the IEEE South Africa Section in collaboration with the IEEE Rwanda Subsection. The event will be hosted at GIMPA, Kigali, Rwanda from the 18th to 20th of November 2026, under the theme “Innovating Africa’s Future Through Technology”.

This conference will feature world-class plenary speakers, technical symposiums, industry and academic panels, workshops, tutorials, and invited tracks on emerging technologies. IEEE AFRICON 2026 is co-sponsored by IEEE Region 8 and the IEEE South Africa Section and is the flagship conference for the African continent.

This will be the 18th Edition of the biennial conference which was previously held in Nairobi, Kenya (1983), Abidjan, Ivory Coast (1987), Mbabane, Swaziland (1992), Stellenbosch, South Africa (1996), Cape Town, South Africa (1999), George, South Africa (2002), Gaborone, Botswana (2004), Windhoek, Namibia (2007), Nairobi, Kenya (2009), Livingston, Zambia (2011), Mauritius (2013), Addis Ababa, Ethiopia (2015), Cape Town, South Africa (2017), Accra, Ghana (2019), Arusha, Tanzania (2021), Nairobi, Kenya (2023), and Polokwane, South Africa (2025).
